Why 5,000 words?

A handful of words does most of the talking.

Word frequency is brutally lopsided. You don't need a dictionary — you need the short list that actually shows up in conversation, learned in the order you'll meet it.

500

The 500 most common words already make up about half of everyday speech. That's your first few weeks.

5,000

At 5,000 words you understand most of what you'll ever hear — the app's entire vocabulary, ranked by real-world frequency.

0 servers

Words are checked by a speech model running on your iPhone. No account, no internet, no one listening in.

How it works

Three steps. One of them is your voice.

01

Find your level

A quick placement test discovers what you already know, so you start exactly where your vocabulary ends — not at "hello" again.

02

Say the word

The app shows a word and opens the mic. Say it. On-device speech recognition confirms it — no typing, no multiple choice, no swiping cards into the void.

03

Keep it forever

Spaced repetition brings each word back right before you'd forget it. A few minutes a day compounds into a working vocabulary.

Built for actually learning

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

Speak, don't swipe

Every word is an out-loud rep. If you can say it on the spot, you own it — recognition beats recall every time.

Ranked by frequency

Words arrive in the order the language actually uses them, so every session raises the share of speech you understand.

Spaced repetition

Reviews land at the moment you're about to forget. The schedule is the app's problem, not yours.

100% offline

The speech model lives on your phone. Planes, metros, mountaintops, dead zones — all study time.

Hints, not answers

Stuck on a word? Reveal it letter by letter. You keep momentum without the answer being handed to you.

Dark. Quiet. Focused.

No ads, no mascot guilt-tripping you, no confetti economy. Just you, the mic, and the next word.

Private by architecture

Your voice never leaves your phone.

Speech recognition runs entirely on-device. Recordings are transcribed in place and immediately discarded — nothing is uploaded, stored, or used for training. Not even we can hear you practice.

The boring details are in the privacy policy. It's short, because there's not much to collect.
